The year data centers went from backend to center stage

Activist groups, numbering 142 across 24 states, are organizing against new data center developments due to environmental and potential health impacts, controversial AI usage, and rising local electricity bills. This populist uprising is a response to the industry's rapid growth, with construction spending skyrocketing 331% since 2021, totaling hundreds of billions of dollars. Being Santa Claus is a year-round calling

요약

A study published in the Academy of Management Journal reveals that professional Santas often view their role as a year-round calling, maintaining aspects of the identity beyond the holiday season. Researchers, including Christina Hymer, analyzed surveys from 849 professional Santas and conducted over 50 personal interviews. They identified three categories of professional Santas: prototypical (straight, portly white men with natural white beards), semi-prototypical (fitting some but not all traditional physical characteristics), and those who don't fit the stereotypical image. The study explores the motivations and experiences of individuals who choose this line of work.

Is the Internet's time wrong? NIST warns servers may have lapsed due to a power outage

요약

The National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) has issued an alert warning that some of its public time servers may be unreliable due to a prolonged utility power outage at its Boulder, Colorado campus. A generator failure interrupted the distribution of America’s primary atomic time scale, causing some NIST servers to quietly serve inaccurate timestamps while still responding to network requests. The advisory focuses on specific time-x-b.nist.gov addresses and the authenticated ntp-b.nist.gov service. To prevent the spread of incorrect data, NIST may temporarily take affected hosts offline. Round robin DNS protected many users from localized time failures.
