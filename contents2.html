The enterprise voice AI split: Why architecture — not model quality — defines your compliance posture

요약

Enterprise decision-makers in voice AI have historically faced a trade-off between "Native" speech-to-speech (S2S) models for speed and emotional fidelity, and "Modular" stacks for control and auditability. This choice has evolved into a governance and compliance decision as voice agents move into regulated workflows. Google, with Gemini 2.5 Flash and 3.0 Flash, has commoditized the "raw intelligence" layer, making voice automation economically viable. OpenAI responded with a 20% price cut on its Realtime API. Concurrently, a "Unified" modular architecture is emerging, with providers like Together AI co-locating voice stack components to achieve native-like speed while maintaining audit trails and intervention points required by regulated industries. These developments are collapsing the traditional trade-off between speed and control, making the decision for executives a strategic choice between a cost-efficient, generalized utility model and a domain-specific, vertically integrated stack that supports compliance.

Quintero's search, aided by media and his own coding instincts, culminated in a viral LinkedIn post revealing the mystery.
