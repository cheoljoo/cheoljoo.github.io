Polymarket continues its partnership spree with a Major League Baseball deal

요약

Polymarket, a prediction platform, has entered into a new partnership with Major League Baseball (MLB), becoming its official prediction market partner. This deal grants Polymarket exclusive access to official league data and the right to use MLB logos, along with brand exposure at games and through the league’s digital channels. Concurrently, MLB and the Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC) signed a memo of understanding to share information related to baseball and prediction markets, addressing previous MLB concerns about the impact of such markets on sports audiences. This partnership is seen by MLB as a means to enforce integrity standards. Polymarket has been expanding its operations since September, when the CFTC eased regulatory restrictions, leading to data partnerships with companies like Google and Palantir, and sports partnerships with Major League Soccer and Major League Hockey.

OpenAI is acquiring open source Python tool-maker Astral

요약

OpenAI has announced its acquisition of Astral, a company known for developing popular open-source Python tools like uv, Ruff, and ty. The Astral team will be integrated into OpenAI's Codex team. While the financial terms of the deal were not disclosed, OpenAI stated that the acquisition aims to accelerate its work on Codex and enhance AI capabilities across the software development lifecycle. The integration of Astral's tools is expected to enable AI agents to interact more directly with developer tools. Astral's projects, including the Rust-based Python package manager uv, the Python linter and formatter Ruff, and the fast Python type-checker ty, have garnered significant monthly downloads. Charlie Marsh, Astral's founder, confirmed that OpenAI will continue to support these open-source tools post-acquisition.

Android will hide some app sideloading behind a new one-time security process

요약

Android is introducing a new, multi-step security process for sideloading apps from unverified developers, a policy that has been softened since its initial proposal in November 2025. This process will require average users to enable developer mode, confirm security risks, restart their phone, wait a day, and verify their identity before installing such apps. Users can choose to enable this ability for seven days or indefinitely, but will always receive a warning. Additionally, Google will offer free "limited distribution accounts" for hobbyist developers, allowing them to share apps with up to 20 devices without requiring government ID or registration fees. Google justifies these changes as security measures, comparing developer requirements to airport ID checks.
