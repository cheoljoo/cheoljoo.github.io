Creative suite company Canva launched its own design model on Thursday that understands different layers and formats today to power its features. The platform is now making that assistant available across screens, including the design and elements tabs. Earlier this year, the company added a spreadsheet product and a feature that lets users create mini apps through prompts. Now it’s connecting these two products, allowing users to make use of data stored in the spreadsheet and create widgets from that.

Canva hopes to position itself at the center of this shift with a sweeping new suite of tools. At the heart of Canva’s strategy is its Creative Operating System (COS) underlying. This “engine,” as Adams describes it, integrates documents, websites, presentations, sheets, whiteboards, videos, social content, hundreds of millions of photos, illustrations, a rich sound library, and numerous templates, charts, and branded elements. The COS is getting a 2.0 upgrade, but the crucial advance is the “middle, crucial layer” that fully integrates AI and makes it accessible throughout various workflows, Adams explained. This gives creative and technical teams a single dashboard for generating, editing and launching all types of content.

2024’s Startup Battlefield runner-up geCKo Materials reveals four new products at TechCrunch Disrupt

요약

geCKo Materials, the 2024 TechCrunch Disrupt Startup Battlefield runner-up, unveiled four new products at this year's event, expanding its commercialization efforts. Founder Dr. Capella Kerst introduced a semiconductor wafer handling tool, a robotic gripper for smooth surfaces, a curved robotic "end effector" for irregular shapes, and an all-purpose robotic arm gripper. Inspired by lizard feet, geCKo's dry adhesive is described as a residue-free, quick-attach/detach, and non-electrical alternative to Velcro. A one-inch tile can hold 16 pounds and endure up to 120,000 attachments, functioning for varying durations. The technology's adaptability has attracted major clients like Ford, NASA, and Pacific Gas & Electric. Since last year's competition, geCKo has tripled its team, secured an $8 million fundraise, and seen its adhesive used in six space missions, demonstrating its versatility across environments, including vacuums. Kerst showcased a Fanuc robotic arm utilizing geCKo tiles for object manipulation and presented videos of its commercial applications, including faster and safer semiconductor wafer movement compared to existing suction or vacuum technologies.
