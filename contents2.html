Channel Surfer lets you watch YouTube like it’s old-school cable TV

요약

Channel Surfer is a new web app by London-based developer Steven Irby that allows users to watch YouTube videos in an interface resembling a retro cable TV guide. The app features 40 topic-focused channels, including news, politics, sports, lifestyle, music, and tech-related content like AI & ML, Code & Dev, Space, Retro Tech, Tech & Gadgets, and Gaming. Users can browse channels, tune into videos mid-stream, and view upcoming programming for the next 24 hours. This experience mimics old-school live television, a popular feature on free streaming services. Irby created Channel Surfer to combat algorithm fatigue and indecision when choosing what to watch on YouTube, aiming to provide a more relaxed, channel-surfing experience.

Agents don't replace vector search - they make it harder to get right

요약

The role of vector databases in the agentic AI world is a growing concern. While some argued that large language models with expanded context windows would make purpose-built vector search obsolete, production evidence suggests the opposite. Qdrant, an open-source vector search company, recently announced a significant Series B funding, indicating that the retrieval problem has scaled up and become more complex with the advent of AI agents. Qdrant's CEO, Andre Zayarni, highlights that agents generate hundreds or thousands of queries per second, a significant increase compared to human queries, which necessitates a robust retrieval layer that context windows cannot fully replace. Agents require high-recall search across continuously changing proprietary data, maintaining retrieval quality, and sustaining high query volumes for autonomous decision-making. Most AI memory frameworks currently utilize some form of vector storage, underscoring the continued importance of vector databases.

Buy now or wait? SD card prices are shooting through the roof – here’s how to navigate the ‘memory crisis’

요약

SD and microSD card prices have significantly increased in 2026 due to a 'global memory crisis,' a combination of factors including an 'AI memory tax,' production shifts, strategic underproduction, and rising component costs. For example, a SanDisk 128GB Extreme PRO SDXC card, which cost under £20 last Black Friday (November 28, 2025), now costs around £50, representing a 100% increase. Price hikes for high-speed, high-capacity SD cards are even more extreme, reaching up to 300%, with many models also facing stock shortages. Industry analysts predict a gloomy outlook for memory prices.
