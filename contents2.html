Scientists engineer erbium molecular qubits that plug directly into existing fiber networks, promising telecom-ready quantum systems

요약

Scientists have engineered erbium-based molecular qubits that can directly interface quantum systems with existing fiber networks, paving the way for telecom-ready quantum systems. These qubits exhibit precise optical and spin transitions, allowing operations at standard telecommunications wavelengths and enabling magnetic quantum states to be controlled and read out using fiber-optic compatible light. This development, led by the University of Chicago in collaboration with other national laboratories and universities, could support scalable quantum network development without requiring entirely new communication hardware.

Microreactor startup Antares raises $96M for land, sea, and space-based nuclear power

요약

Nuclear startup Antares secured $96 million in Series B funding ($71 million equity, $25 million debt) to advance its small modular reactor design. The round was led by Shine Capital with participation from Alt Capital, Caffeinated, FiftyThree Stations, and Industrious, among others. Antares' R1 microreactor, which uses TRISO fuel (carbon- and ceramic-coated uranium spheres in graphite), targets commercial, defense, and space applications, producing 100 kilowatts to 1 megawatt of electricity. This funding reflects a recent surge in interest and investment in nuclear power. Other companies in the sector have also seen significant capital raises: Amazon-backed X-energy recently raised a $700 million Series D round, following a $700 million Series C. Deep Fission went public via a $30 million reverse merger. Aalo Atomics raised $100 million to build a microreactor-powered data center, and Nvidia contributed to a $650 million round for TerraPower, a Bill Gates-backed SMR startup. Even large nuclear plants are experiencing a resurgence.

Amazon's new AI can code for days without human help. What does that mean for software engineers?

요약

Amazon Web Services (AWS) announced "frontier agents," a new class of AI systems capable of autonomous work for hours or days without human intervention, aiming to automate the entire software development lifecycle. The announcement was made by AWS CEO Matt Garman at the re:Invent conference on December 2, 2025. These agents include Kiro for software development, AWS Security Agent for application security, and AWS DevOps Agent for IT operations. Amazon views these as a significant leap beyond existing AI coding tools like GitHub Copilot or CodeWhisperer, which require constant human interaction. Deepak Singh, VP of developer agents and experiences at Amazon, stated that frontier agents are designed for complex, multi-day challenges, allowing them to think, try solutions, and reach conclusions independently. This move highlights Amazon's ambition to lead in developing AI systems for intricate, multi-step engineering tasks.
