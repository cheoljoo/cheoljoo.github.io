But a new paper released by Google Research suggests that we may have been overthinking it. The researchers found that simply repeating the input query—literally copying and pasting the prompt so it appears twice—consistently improves performance across major models including Gemini, GPT-4o, Claude, and DeepSeek. The paper, titled " Prompt Repetition Improves Non-Reasoning LLMs ," released last month just before the holidays, presents a finding that is almost suspiciously simple: for tasks that don’t require complex reasoning steps, stating the prompt twice yields significantly better results than stating it once. Bye Bye MLC: QLC and TLC to be dominant SSD technologies amidst AI's ravenous appetite for NAND — and PLC won't probably happen till the era of Petabyte SSDs

요약

The NAND Flash memory market is shifting, with Multi-Level Cell (MLC) NAND moving away from mainstream storage to specialized niches like industrial, automotive, medical, and networking equipment. This decline in MLC capacity, projected to be over 40% year-over-year in 2026, is driven by the increasing dominance of Triple-Level Cell (TLC) and Quad-Level Cell (QLC) technologies in consumer and enterprise storage. The change is fueled by AI's growing demand for NAND, pushing the industry towards higher-density, more cost-efficient solutions. While MLC's endurance and predictable behavior are still valued in specific sectors, the broader market is embracing TLC and QLC, with Penta-Level Cell (PLC) likely not emerging until the era of petabyte SSDs.
