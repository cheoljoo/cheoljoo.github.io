The AI agent bottleneck isn't model performance — it's permissions

요약

Enterprise AI agents are facing a significant bottleneck not due to model performance, but rather due to permissioning issues. The core challenge lies in defining what an AI agent is authorized to access, on whose behalf, and how the system enforces these permissions. Workday addresses this by leveraging its existing system of record as the governance layer for agents, aiming to ensure the integrity of approvals and security models. Workday's president for product and technology, Gerrit Kazmaier, highlights that customers often struggle with DIY AI solutions that compromise security. Workday's Sana agent system, launched in March, has expanded its partnership with Google to integrate with Gemini Enterprise, making Sana-built agents discoverable there. A major hurdle in developing these agents was ensuring accuracy, particularly for HR and finance applications, where even minor errors can have significant consequences and correction loops are often absent. Workday tackled this by building Gemini as its base reasoning layer, complemented by its context engine and business process logic.

The new report is based on 2024 data and compares the US to 19 countries, including many in Europe, as well as Australia, Canada, Chile, Israel, Japan, Korea, Mexico, New Zealand, Turkey, and the United Kingdom. The report highlighted critical weaknesses in the US healthcare system, including having the fewest primary care providers of all countries in the analysis. The US has 0.3 primary care providers per 1,000 people, while the overall average is 1.1 providers per 1,000, and the highest-ranking countries, Australia and the Netherlands, have 1.8. While the US has a higher maternal death rate than any other country in the study, at nearly 19 deaths per 100,000 live births in 2023, maternal mortality for Black women in the US is 50 deaths per 100,000. The average of all the countries was 9.5, with 11 countries having maternal death rates at less than 5 per 100,000 live births. The report notes that the US uniquely lacks universal health coverage among high-income peer countries. Overall, other countries have already come up with strategies to address the failings seen in the US health system, including reducing healthcare costs, strengthening primary care, and addressing inequities.
