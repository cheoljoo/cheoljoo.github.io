The new report is based on 2024 data and compares the US to 19 countries, including many in Europe, as well as Australia, Canada, Chile, Israel, Japan, Korea, Mexico, New Zealand, Turkey, and the United Kingdom. The report highlighted critical weaknesses in the US healthcare system, including having the fewest primary care providers of all countries in the analysis. The US has 0.3 primary care providers per 1,000 people, while the overall average is 1.1 providers per 1,000, and the highest-ranking countries, Australia and the Netherlands, have 1.8. While the US has a higher maternal death rate than any other country in the study, at nearly 19 deaths per 100,000 live births in 2023, maternal mortality for Black women in the US is 50 deaths per 100,000. The average of all the countries was 9.5, with 11 countries having maternal death rates at less than 5 per 100,000 live births. The report notes that the US uniquely lacks universal health coverage among high-income peer countries. Overall, other countries have already come up with strategies to address the failings seen in the US health system, including reducing healthcare costs, strengthening primary care, and addressing inequities. In more good news for Amazon, Snowflake signs $6B deal with AWS for AI CPU chips

요약

Cloud data storage company Snowflake has signed a new $6 billion, five-year agreement with Amazon Web Services (AWS). This significant deal, nearly matching Snowflake's total sales via AWS Marketplace since 2012, is driven by accelerating customer spending on AWS, particularly due to AI initiatives. Snowflake offers Cortex AI, an AI building tool that leverages enterprise data for features like text-based database queries and summary reports. A key aspect of this contract is Snowflake gaining more access to AWS's Graviton ARM-based CPU chips. These chips are crucial for AI tasks beyond training and reasoning, such as automation via agents, where CPU usage dramatically increases. Amazon CEO Andy Jassy has highlighted the better price-performance of their homegrown AI chips compared to Nvidia's, even though AWS still utilizes Nvidia's offerings. The high demand for AI processing is leading cloud providers to rapidly deploy chips, and while many AI models are architected for Nvidia, Amazon's own chips provide a more cost-effective deployment option for the cloud giant.
