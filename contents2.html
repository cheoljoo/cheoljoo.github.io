California lawmakers are working on a bill to preserve access to online games

요약

California lawmakers are advancing a bill, the "Protect Our Games Act," aimed at preserving access to online games after publishers cease active support. Advised by the games preservation group Stop Killing Games, the proposed law would require game publishers to provide a 60-day warning before essential online services are terminated. Publishers would then need to offer a full refund, a patch to make the game playable offline, or a version independent of their servers. This bill, which would apply to titles released from January 1, 2027, onwards and exclude free or subscription-based games, addresses the issue of modern game ownership where reliance on server infrastructure can render purchased games unplayable once support ends.
